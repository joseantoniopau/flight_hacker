A free tool that runs on your own computer. It searches Google Flights AND the world of airline miles redemptions at the same time, then tells you the cheapest way to book — whether that's paying cash or using points you already have.

What it does, in plain English

When you search for a flight on Google, you see cash prices. That's it. This tool adds everything Google doesn't:

  • 💸 Award miles search. Checks if you can book the same flight with miles from 22+ programs (United, Delta, American, Aeroplan, Virgin Atlantic, Qatar, ANA, etc.) and tells you the dollar value of the redemption.
  • 💳 Knows your points balance. If you have Chase, Amex, Citi, Capital One, or Bilt points, it figures out which airlines you can transfer them to and how many miles you'd have.
  • ✈️ Nearby airports. Type "MIA" and it also searches Fort Lauderdale and Palm Beach automatically. Type "JFK" and it adds LaGuardia and Newark. Those flights are often hundreds cheaper.
  • 🧠 Travel-hacking moves. Positioning flights (fly to a cheaper hub first), hidden-city tickets (with safety warnings), free stopovers, open-jaw trips.
  • 📰 Mistake fare alerts. Watches 9 travel-deal websites and shows when an airline accidentally publishes a $300 business class ticket.
  • ⏰ Set-and-forget watchlists. Save a route + your budget. Your computer checks every hour and texts you when prices drop.
  • 🎯 Curated sweet spots. A handpicked list of 25 amazing miles deals (like "Tokyo first class on ANA for 110K miles" instead of the usual 200K).

How to install (10 minutes, no coding required)

What you need first

  • A Mac or Linux computer (Windows users see the bottom)
  • ~10 minutes of patience for the one-time setup
  • A Seats.aero Pro subscription — $99/year at seats.aero (this is what powers the miles search; without it you only get cash prices)
  • (Optional) A Telegram account if you want price-drop alerts on your phone

  1. Open Terminal

    On a Mac: press Cmd + Space, type "Terminal", press Enter. A black window opens. That's where you'll paste the commands below.

  2. Check you have Python (most Macs do)

    Copy-paste this into Terminal and press Enter:

    python3 --version

    If you see Python 3.10 or higher, you're good. If not, download from python.org/downloads first.

  3. Download the tool

    git clone https://github.com/joseantoniopau/flight_hacker ~/Desktop/flight-hacker
cd ~/Desktop/flight-hacker

    This downloads everything to a folder called flight-hacker on your Desktop.

  4. Run the installer

    ./install.sh

    Takes about 30 seconds. Sets up the tool, installs the dependencies, and creates a hourly background job for your watchlists.

  5. Add your Seats.aero key

    After you sign up at seats.aero, they'll give you a key that starts with pro_xxxxx.

    ./setup-keys.sh

    When it asks for the Seats.aero key, paste it in and press Enter. Press Enter to skip everything else.

  6. Tell it your miles balances

    This is what makes the tool know which redemptions you can actually book. Open this file with any text editor:

    ~/Desktop/flight-hacker/data/user_balances.json

    You'll see a template. Replace the numbers with your actual balances (log into your credit card + airline accounts to check). Save the file.

  7. Launch the tool

    python3 ui/server.py

    You'll see Uvicorn running on http://127.0.0.1:8721. Leave this Terminal window open.

    Now open your web browser and go to:

    http://127.0.0.1:8721

    The tool is running. ✨

How to use it

  1. Click "SEARCH" in the sidebar.

    The first thing on the left.

  2. Type your departure airport.

    Like "JFK" or "Miami". Then your destination.

  3. Pick your travel dates.

    Depart, return. Toggle "One-way" if no return.

  4. Click "Search flights".

    Wait ~10–20 seconds. You'll see a table of options.

  5. Look at the "Total" column.

    That's the true cost — whether you pay cash or use miles. Sorted cheapest first.

  6. Click "Book" on any row.

    Goes directly to the airline's website. Book there.

Other things you can do

  • Save a route as a watchlist. After a search, click "Save as watch". Your computer will check that route every hour and ping you when the price drops below your threshold.
  • Browse mistake fares. Click "MISTAKE FARES" in the sidebar to see deals like $400 business class to Europe.
  • See sweet spots. Click "SWEET SPOTS" — 25 hand-curated great miles deals. Click any row to see if your points cover it.
  • Set your balances and budget. The BALANCES section lets you update your card + airline points anytime.

Tomorrow and after

You only install once. After that, to use it again:

  1. Open Terminal
  2. Type: cd ~/Desktop/flight-hacker && python3 ui/server.py
  3. Open http://127.0.0.1:8721 in your browser

To stop it: in the Terminal window, press Ctrl + C.

Privacy and safety

  • ✅ Runs on your own computer. Nothing is uploaded anywhere. The tool just makes API calls (Google Flights, Seats.aero) like your browser does when you visit those sites.
  • ✅ Your miles balances stay in a file on your computer. You're not sharing credit card numbers — just point totals.
  • ✅ No tracking, no ads, no cookies. Unlike Google, this tool doesn't tie your searches to your identity.
  • ✅ Booking happens on the airline's site. The tool finds the cheapest path and links you out — it never touches your payment info.
  • ⚠️ Hidden-city tickets: Legal but airlines don't like them. The tool always shows a clear TOS-RISK badge and full warnings. You decide.

Troubleshooting

I get an error running ./install.sh
Make sure Python 3.10 or newer is installed. Type python3 --version to check.
The website doesn't load at localhost:8721
Make sure the Terminal window is still open and shows "Uvicorn running...". If you closed it, run python3 ui/server.py again from the flight-hacker folder.
Search is taking a long time
First searches take 10–30 seconds because we're checking multiple airports and miles programs in parallel. Later searches with the same route are cached and instant.
My miles balances aren't being used
Open ~/Desktop/flight-hacker/data/user_balances.json, edit the numbers, save the file. Refresh the web page.
I don't see award (miles) results
Verify your Seats.aero key is set: run cat ~/Desktop/flight-hacker/.env and check that SEATS_AERO_API_KEY=pro_xxxxx is filled in.
I'm on Windows
Install WSL (Windows Subsystem for Linux) from Microsoft's instructions, then follow the Mac steps inside WSL.

What this does NOT do

  • ❌ Does not book the ticket for you. Shows you the cheapest path and links to the airline.
  • ❌ Does not touch your credit card. You only enter points balances (totally separate from card numbers).
  • ❌ Does not track you. Everything is local.
  • ❌ Does not help with hotels, cars, or cruises — just flights.
